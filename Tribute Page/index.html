Flavius Belisarius

The Last Roman

Belisarius as portrayed in a mosaic at the Basilica of San Vatale, now in modern-day Ravenna, Italy.

Timeline of Belisarius

  • 505 C.E. Born in Germane in the the province of Thracia-Illyria (between Bulgaria and Turkey)
  • 520 C.E. Leads the Bucellarii regiment of Germanic Heavy Cavalry, becoming Emperor Justin I's favorite bodyguard
  • 527 C.E. Emperor Justinian appoints Belisarius as Commander of the fortress at Dara
  • 530 C.E. Belisarius wins a decisive victory against Perozes of Sassanid Persia in the Battle of Dara
  • 532 C.E. Belisarius and Mundus lead their personal legions to suppress the Nika Riots
  • 533 C.E. Belisarius defeats King Gelimer at the Battles of Ad Decimum and Tricamarum, conquering North Africa from the Vandals
  • 534 C.E. Emperor Justinian recalls Belisarius back to Constantinople, where he is granted a triumph, bringing the captured Gelimer in the streets for the parade
  • 535 C.E. Justinian gives Belisarius the Office of Consul in the Roman Senate
  • 536 C.E. After dealing with a mutiny in North Africa, Belisarius leads 7,500 in Expedition to reconquer Italy from the Ostrogoths, capturing Sicily, Naples, and Rome.
  • 539 C.E. Belisarius refuses the Ostrogothic offer to become their King, and captures Ravenna and King Vitiges.
  • 540 C.E. Belisarius is recalled by Justinian to fight off an invasion by the Sassanid Emperor Khosrow II.
  • 545 C.E. Belisarius leads 4,500 back to Italy against the Ostrogothic King Totilla.
  • 559 C.E. Belisarius leads an outnumbered Roman force and defeats the Bulgars.
  • 562 C.E. Belisarius is falsely charged with corruption, but is pardoned by Justinian and restored to full power in his court.
  • 565 C.E. Belisarius and Justinian die of natural causes the same year.
